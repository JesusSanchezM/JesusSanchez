Customer Retention Analytics

Predictive churn modeling and economic impact for a telecom operator

📌 Context

A telecommunications company faced a 15% increase in customer cancellation rate during the last quarter. The Commercial Director needed to identify who was at risk of leaving and what factors were driving the churn, in order to design effective retention campaigns and measure the economic impact.

Business question: Which customers are most likely to churn in the next month, and why?

🎯 Objective

  • Identify customers at risk of churn at least 3 weeks in advance.
  • Pinpoint the variables that most influence cancellation decisions.
  • Prioritize retention actions focused on the highest-value segments.
  • Estimate potential revenue savings from preventing churn.

🛠️ Methodology

Exploration & Data

  • Exploratory analysis of 7,043 customers with 33 variables (demographics, services, billing).
  • Cleaning and treatment of outliers and missing values.
  • Feature engineering: tenure, ticket ratio, average charges.

Modeling & Interpretation

  • Model comparison: Random Forest, XGBoost, Logistic Regression.
  • Hyperparameter tuning with GridSearchCV.
  • Interpretability with SHAP to identify churn drivers.
  • Customer segmentation by risk profile.

Key result: The final model achieved an 82% Recall in identifying customers who churned, outperforming the baseline model by 12% and reducing false negatives.

💡 Business Insights

Monthly contracts

Customers with monthly contracts are 3.2 times more likely to churn than those with annual contracts.

Technical support

Customers who have never used technical support have a 40% higher churn rate than those who have had at least one interaction.

Tenure

65% of churn occurs among customers with less than 12 months of tenure.

High monthly charges

Customers with monthly charges above $100 USD have a churn risk 1.8 times higher than the average.

📌 Executive Recommendations

  • Offer discounts before month 6: Retention campaigns focused on new customers to build loyalty during the critical period.
  • Include free support: Packages that include priority technical support for monthly contract customers.
  • Migrate to annual contracts: Incentives for high-value customers to switch to annual plans (e.g., free months).
  • Segment campaigns: Send personalized promotions only to the top 20% of at-risk customers to optimize budget.
Estimated impact: 12% reduction in churn rate within the first 6 months, representing ~$1.2M USD in saved annual revenue.
