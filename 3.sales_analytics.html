📌 Context

An online store with over 96,000 orders needed to better understand customer behavior to optimize pricing, promotions, and inventory management. The commercial team wanted to identify who are the most profitable buyers, which products drive sales, and how customer retention evolves over time.

Business questions: Which customer segments generate the most value? How do sales behave by season? Which products have the highest turnover and margin?

🎯 Objective

  • Segment customers by value using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary).
  • Analyze customer retention through monthly cohorts.
  • Identify product performance and classify inventory with ABC analysis.
  • Propose data-driven commercial actions to increase average ticket and loyalty.

🛠️ Methodology

Exploration & Data

  • Extraction and cleaning of transactional data using SQL (PostgreSQL).
  • Exploratory analysis of sales, products, categories, and customers.
  • Calculation of key metrics: average ticket, purchase frequency, tenure.

Modeling & Segmentation

  • RFM segmentation with NTILE and CASE to classify customers into VIP, Big Spenders, Core, and At Risk.
  • Cohort retention analysis using DATE_TRUNC and window functions.
  • ABC inventory classification based on revenue contribution (80/15/5 rule).

Key finding: 20% of customers (Champions + Big Spenders) generate ~65% of total revenue, and customers who make a second purchase within 30 days have 50% higher 12‑month retention.

💡 Business Insights

🏆 Top Customers

20% of customers (Champions & Big Spenders) contribute ~65% of total revenue.

📅 Seasonality

Sales peak in November and December, driven by electronics and fashion categories (40% increase vs. average).

🔄 Cohort Retention

Customers who make a second purchase within 30 days have 50% higher retention at 12 months.

⭐ Star Products

Category A products (top 20% of categories) account for 80% of revenue and 75% of margin.

📌 Executive Recommendations

  • Loyalty program: Focus promotions and discounts on the Top segment to increase their average ticket and frequency.
  • Re‑engage at‑risk customers: Send re‑engagement campaigns to customers who haven't purchased in over 90 days (At Risk segment).
  • Optimize inventory: Prioritize stock of Category A products (high margin and turnover) and reduce investment in Category C.
  • Leverage seasonality: Plan marketing campaigns and promotions in peak months (October–December).
Estimated impact: 8% increase in the Top segment's average ticket and a 15% reduction in obsolete inventory, generating an estimated ~$500K USD increase in annual margin.
