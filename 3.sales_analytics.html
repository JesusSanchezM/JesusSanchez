Sales Analytics

Customer segmentation, cohort retention, and commercial performance analysis for an e‑commerce

📌 Context

An online store with over 10,000 monthly transactions needed to better understand customer behavior to optimize pricing, promotions, and inventory management. The commercial team wanted to identify who are the most profitable buyers, which products drive sales, and how customer retention evolves over time.

Business questions: Which customer segments generate the most value? How do sales behave by season? Which products have the highest turnover and margin?

🎯 Objective

  • Segment customers by value (RFM: Recency, Frequency, Monetary).
  • Analyze customer retention through monthly cohorts.
  • Identify seasonality patterns and sales trends by category.
  • Propose data-driven commercial actions to increase average ticket and loyalty.

🛠️ Methodology

Exploration & Data

  • Extraction and cleaning of transactional data (SQL) from a relational database.
  • Exploratory analysis of sales, products, categories, and customers.
  • Calculation of key metrics: average ticket, purchase frequency, tenure.

Modeling & Segmentation

  • RFM segmentation to classify customers into categories (Top, Loyal, New, At Risk, etc.).
  • Cohort analysis to measure retention at 3, 6, and 12 months.
  • Seasonality and trend analysis with moving windows.
  • Identification of products with highest margin and turnover (ABC).

Key finding: 20% of customers (Top segment) generate 65% of revenue, and 6‑month retention is 35% higher for customers who receive personalized promotions.

💡 Business Insights

Top Segment

20% of customers (frequent, high-value buyers) contribute 65% of total revenue.

Seasonality

Sales increase by 40% in Q4 (November–December), driven by electronics and fashion categories.

Cohort Retention

Customers who make a second purchase within the first 30 days have 50% higher 12‑month retention than average.

Star Products

10% of products (Category A) account for 70% of sales and 80% of margin.

📌 Executive Recommendations

  • Loyalty program: Focus promotions and discounts on the Top segment to increase their average ticket and frequency.
  • Re‑engage at‑risk customers: Send re‑engagement campaigns to customers who haven't purchased in over 90 days ("At Risk" segment).
  • Optimize inventory: Prioritize stock of Category A products (high margin and turnover) and reduce investment in Category C.
  • Leverage seasonality: Plan marketing campaigns and promotions in peak months (October–December).
Estimated impact: 8% increase in the Top segment's average ticket and a 15% reduction in obsolete inventory, generating an estimated ~$500K USD increase in annual margin.
