Executive BI Dashboard

Interactive dashboard for strategic financial and operational performance monitoring

📌 Context

The senior leadership of a multinational company needed a centralized tool to monitor the financial and operational performance of its various business units. Monthly Excel reports were tedious to update, did not allow filtering by region or product, and made it difficult to detect deviations early. An executive dashboard was required to consolidate key KPIs, enable variance analysis (YoY, MoM), and facilitate rapid decision-making.

Business questions: How is gross margin evolving by business unit? Which regions are underperforming against budget? Which products have the highest profitability and volume?

🎯 Objective

  • Design an interactive dashboard in Tableau that consolidates financial and operational data.
  • Enable variance analysis (month-over-month, year-over-year) with segmentation by region, product, and channel.
  • Visualize trends in revenue, costs, margin, and profitability.
  • Facilitate effective communication of results to the board of directors.

🛠️ Methodology

Design & Data

  • Extraction and consolidation of data from multiple sources (ERP, CRM, spreadsheets).
  • Definition of strategic KPIs: Revenue, Gross Margin, EBITDA, ROI, Inventory Turnover.
  • Preparation of a data model in Tableau with joins and calculated fields.

Visualization & Interaction

  • Creation of dashboards with line charts, bar charts, maps, and detail tables.
  • Implementation of dynamic filters for region, product, period, and business unit.
  • Incorporation of storytelling: titles, annotations, and highlights of significant variances.

Key result: The dashboard reduced report generation time from 3 days to 30 minutes, and allowed executives to identify a margin decline in the northern region 2 months before it impacted annual results.

💡 Business Insights

Northern region margin

Gross margin fell 8% in the last 3 months due to an unexpected increase in logistics costs.

Star products

20% of products generate 75% of total margin, concentrating commercial effort on these lines.

Sales seasonality

Online channel sales grow 30% during Q4, while the physical channel remains stable.

ROI by unit

The electronics business unit has the highest ROI (25%), while furniture is below 10%.

📊 Interactive Dashboard

The dashboard was developed in Tableau and allows executives to explore financial performance at global, regional, and product levels. It includes executive summary views, variance analysis, and transactional details on demand.

📌 Executive Recommendations

  • Review logistics costs in the northern region: Renegotiate supplier contracts or seek alternative transportation to recover margin.
  • Focus investment on high-margin products: Increase marketing and promotion budgets for the most profitable lines.
  • Strengthen the online channel: Leverage Q4 seasonality with specific campaigns for the digital channel.
  • Evaluate furniture business unit viability: Analyze whether to restructure or discontinue low-ROI lines.
Estimated impact: Implementation of the dashboard and derived actions resulted in a 5% improvement in gross margin the following year, equivalent to $2.5M USD in additional value.
